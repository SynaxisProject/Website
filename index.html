Our first project:

Question Quest Interviews

The Orthodox Church's tradition and doctrinal riches are profound. It's time to open the treasure chest: In a few short films, we want to distill the distinctly Orthodox answers to some of the most simple yet haunting questions we face in our lives.

Why am I here? Is there a God and how can I know it? How should I live? Do I matter? What does it mean to love? ...

While the questions might be simple, to answer them to some degree of satisfaction deep wisdom and experience is needed. We will film interviews with Orthodox theologians, priests, and lay people, ask them the same questions, and then produce a short film (2-5 minutes) for each topic. The idea is not to lay out a complete exposition, but to provoke people to think and engage with the questions. The goal is to create something sharable on various social networks, so the clips will be made freely available on YouTube and Vimeo.

About the Synaxis Project

Today, almost no message gets communicated without a cutting edge video clip taking center stage. I myself have definitely gotten used to this. Whenever I explore a topic, look for a product, or check the news, the first thing I do, is click on that ubiquitous YouTube play button. Immediately, I'm immersed in a world that someone has carefully crafted to convey a message. It's powerful stuff.

But let's combine powers: I'm awed by the beauty and elegance of the Orthodox Church. In many conversations and readings about Orthodoxy I've found answers that reveal depths unmatched anywhere else. Unfortunately, very little is available in a format that speaks to the YouTube generation. Being an Orthodox Christian myself, I want to see more of that clear and profound message captured in film.

The idea of the Synaxis Project is to create a place where we gather to collaborate on individual projects to further that cause. Our first pilot project is a set of interviews, depending on how that goes we'll tackle the next project; ideas we have many.

I hope you're in.
-Luke

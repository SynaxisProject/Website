Our first project:

Question Quest Interviews

The Orthodox Church's tradition and doctrinal riches are profound. It's time to open the treasure chest: In a few short films, we want to distill the distinctly Orthodox answers to some of the most simple yet haunting questions we face in our lives.

Why am I here? Is there a God and how can I know it? How should I live? Do I matter? What does it mean to love? ...

While the questions might be simple, to answer them to some degree of satisfaction wisdom and experience is needed. Drawing on the tradition, theology, and live of the Church, we are convinced that Orthodoxy is in a unique position to bring a fresh and crucial perceptive to those questions. We will film interviews with Orthodox theologians, priests, and lay people, ask them the same questions, and then produce a short film (2-5 minutes) for each topic. The goal is not to lay out a complete exposition, but to provoke people to think and engage with the questions. The final product should be something sharable on various social networks, so the clips will be made freely available on YouTube and Vimeo.

About the Synaxis Project

The Syaxis Project is about bringing creatives together for individual projects that strive to capture the beauty and profundity of the Orthodox Church.

We want to use film as our primary medium to communicate, and we want to do it well. While acknowledging that there are things that are not suitable for visual media, because they have to be experienced in person, we think that many aspects of the Church have an aesthetic manifestation that are very presentable in film.

We wish that the end result is accessible to a wide audience and will engage people in a way that they are used to in this digital age: Succinct videos that communicate creatively, while focusing on the essentials.
